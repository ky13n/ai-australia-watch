Clean energy accountability

This project is not about arguing that all AI infrastructure should be stopped. It is about demanding clean energy, transparent planning, water disclosure and enforceable safeguards before major data centre growth is locked in.

Core public ask

New AI and cloud data centres should prove they are powered by additional clean energy, disclose diesel backup and water use, and face cumulative grid and emissions assessment.

  • Require additional new renewables for new data centre demand.
  • Restrict and publicly report diesel backup generator use.
  • Publish facility-level energy and water data.
  • Attach binding clean-energy conditions to fast-tracked approvals.

What the campaign focus is

AI Australia Watch focuses on public-interest safeguards. The central question is not whether AI exists, but whether the infrastructure behind it is being approved with clean energy, water transparency and democratic accountability.

Clean power

Require new data centre demand to be matched by additional renewable energy, not vague claims or existing certificates.

Diesel transparency

Publish backup generator numbers, diesel storage, run hours, emissions and air-quality monitoring, especially near homes and schools.

Water disclosure

Show how much water each facility uses, whether it is potable or recycled, and whether low-water claims are independently verified.

Enforceable policy

Turn government expectations and company sustainability claims into binding approval conditions and contract requirements.
