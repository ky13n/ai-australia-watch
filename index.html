AI Australia Watch

AI Australia Watch is an independent, public-access hub tracking policy and regulatory frameworks, corporate interests, lobbying, infrastructure developments and environmental impacts shaping Australia's digital future.

The focus is not to stop AI development. The focus is to track whether AI systems and the data centre infrastructure behind them are being governed in the public interest, with enforceable clean-energy requirements, water transparency, diesel backup disclosure, privacy protections and democratic accountability.

Energy, Water & Environmental Impacts

AI and cloud infrastructure can create major electricity, water, land-use and backup-power demands. Select a data centre below to review the available energy, water, diesel backup, clean-energy and verification information for that project.

Future AI and cloud data centre proposals should be required to demonstrate additional, local and enforceable renewable energy supply, clear water-use reporting, diesel backup transparency and cumulative grid-impact assessment. Company sustainability claims are treated as claims unless they are site-specific, measurable and enforceable.

Government Policy, Legislation, Lobbying & Regulation

AI development is shaped by laws, procurement decisions, data-sharing rules, planning pathways, industry lobbying and government partnerships. This section tracks the policy settings that determine whether AI and cloud infrastructure serve the public interest.

Digital legislation and regulation

Lobbying and corporate influence
