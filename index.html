AI Australia Watch

AI Australia Watch is an independent, public-access hub tracking infrastructure developments, policy and regulatory frameworks, corporate interests and active lobbying actions shaping Australia's digital future. The platform aggregates public records to map the broad social, political, and environmental landscape surrounding AI expansion across both state and federal levels. The site's purpose is to provide clear information for those looking to help shape technology governance to ensure the safe deployment of automated systems that prioritise democratic accountability, environmental sustainability, and the broader public interest.

Data Centres & Environmental Impacts

This section tracks the physical footprint of AI and cloud data centre infrastructure in Australia, including electricity demand, water use, diesel backup, grid constraints, renewable-energy claims, planning status and environmental governance risk. Select a data centre below to review the available project-level evidence.

Future AI and cloud data centre proposals should be required to demonstrate additional, local and enforceable renewable energy supply, clear water-use reporting, diesel backup transparency and cumulative grid-impact assessment. Company sustainability claims are treated as claims unless they are site-specific, measurable and enforceable.

Environmental governance risk key

MediumMaterial risk remains, but some controls, evidence or lower project scale reduce concern.

Take action and get involved

Democratic technology governance is not limited to experts, companies or government departments. Members of the public can make submissions, respond to consultations, write to elected representatives, contact regulators and ask planning authorities to apply stronger safeguards.

These templates are starting points for people who want to raise environmental, privacy, surveillance, data-rights or AI accountability concerns. Personalise the wording with your own location, experience and the exact name of the consultation, inquiry, project or bill before sending.

Public submissions are more effective when they are specific, respectful and personalised. Add your suburb or state, explain how the issue affects you or your community, and include the exact action you want the decision-maker to take.
