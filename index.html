AI Australia Watch

AI Australia Watch is an independent, public-access hub tracking policy and regulatory frameworks, corporate interests, active lobbying, and infrastructure developments shaping Australia's digital future. The platform aggregates public records to map the broad social, political and environmental landscape surrounding AI expansion across state and federal levels.

Its purpose is to provide clear information for those looking to help shape technology governance and ensure the safe deployment of automated systems that prioritise democratic accountability, environmental sustainability and the broader public interest. AI Australia Watch is non-affiliated, operates free from commercial influence, and holds no alliances, funding ties or attachments to corporate entities or political parties.

Energy, Water & Environmental Impacts

Resource demand, fossil fuel risk and environmental impacts

Click to expand project-level information on electricity demand, water use, diesel backup, renewable energy claims and fossil fuel risk.

As the computational demands of artificial intelligence scale, the physical infrastructure supporting these systems can impose a significant, often obscured toll on Australia's natural resources. This section provides a data-driven log of the material footprint required by both operational and proposed data centres nationwide. By aggregating environmental impact statements, local council planning applications and state utility reports where available, the platform maps electricity demand, water consumption, grid constraints, diesel backup and renewable energy commitments.

Take Action / Get Involved

Current actions, submissions, campaigns and petitions

Click to expand open consultations, official processes, community campaigns and petitions where people can influence technology policy and infrastructure decisions.

Democratic technology governance requires active, informed civic involvement. This section acts as a functional bridge between public-access data and direct democratic participation, providing a centralised toolkit for individuals, community organisations and advocates seeking to influence Australia's digital trajectory.

It lists open federal and state parliamentary inquiries, public consultation projects and official feedback processes where public input can shape policy. Direct links to official portals are provided alongside active grassroots campaigns, advocacy actions and public petitions. Submission templates and additional briefing tools can be added as the project develops.

The campaign frame is clean-energy accountability, democratic oversight and transparent planning, not anti-AI messaging. Public asks focus on additional renewables, diesel reporting, water disclosure, cumulative grid assessment and enforceable safeguards.
